Documentation

The Process

The process of developing this game began with an idea. First, I came up with a simple idea for a web game. After I had a basic concept figured out, I then began to formulate the main content I would include on my website. Only after I had finished my content did I begin working on the design. Before anything, I began with a sketch of what I wanted the site to look like, a template to go off of when actually coding the site. I began building the site with just the page layout in mind. Once I had the page built to the layout I wanted, then I chose to work on the navigation. I have two main sources of navigation on my page. One global navigation system on the top of the page and one local navigation system used exclusively on the proposal page to navigate the sections easier. After finalizing the navigation systems, I then began to choose colors and such to make my page more visually appealing. This was the final step in my process, and once I had established my page's color scheme and design, I was finally finished.

Meeting the Requirements

In order to meet the requirements for this project, I first created my content and submitted it by the beginning of the fourth week of class. For the HTML requirements, I utilized the header and nav tag for the top banner and nav bar on every page. I used footer for the copyright on each page. I used section in the proposal page to denote the actual content of the proposal. I used the article tag for the main text on the landing page and for this documentation page. Each page has a title that denotes that the page is about my game Enter the Wormhole and what that specific subpage is about. All of my positioning and formatting were done in an external CSS. In the CSS, I used ID selectors for the proposal page sidebar, the proposal content on the proposal page, the image on the home page, and the screenshots on the proposal page. My class selector was a wrapper class used for resizing the page. All links on my site have been customized and I used no inline styling. My page was created entirely from scratch and is responsive for browsers as small as 480px wide or smaller.
